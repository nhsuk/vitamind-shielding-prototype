People at higher risk from coronavirus

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

{{ pageTitle }}

Use this service to get free daily vitamin D supplements if you're at high risk (clinically extremely vulnerable) from coronavirus (COVID-19).

Who can use this service

You can use this service if you:

  • have received a letter from the NHS saying that you're at high risk from coronavirus or from the Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC) telling you that you can get supplements
  • are aged [18?] or over
  • live in England

When you apply, we will check that you are on the NHS shielded patients list of people at high risk from coronavirus. If you have received a letter about this, you are on the list.

We will pass your information to a company that will send you 4 months' supply of supplements in January.

You can make 1 application for vitamin D supplements. You have until 4 January 2021 to apply.

Who should not use this service

You do not need these supplements if you:

  • are already taking vitamin D
  • live in a nursing or residential care home - we will send supplements directly to the home where you live

Important: Vitamin D is not suitable for some people

It may not be suitable if:

  • you have [list of conditions] ...
  • you take [list of medicines]...

If you are not sure, read the full safety guidance on GOV.UK or check with a GP.

If you have not received a letter saying that you're at high risk from coronavirus, we cannot send you supplements. If you think you should have got a letter, speak to your GP or hospital care team.

Before you start

You will need to give us some information about yourself, including your NHS number.

Your NHS number is a 10 digit number, like 485 777 3456. You can find it on the letter you have received.

If you are applying for someone else, you need to give us their details so that we can match them to the shielded patients list.
