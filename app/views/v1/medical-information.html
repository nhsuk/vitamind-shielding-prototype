You should not use this service if you:

  • are already taking vitamin D or other supplements that may contain vitamin D, such as cod liver oil, healthy bone supplements or vitamin and mineral supplements
  • are already taking vitamin D that your doctor has prescribed or recommended - continue to take the dose they recommended
  • live in a nursing or residential care home - we will send supplements directly to the home where you live

Medical conditions

If you have any of these conditions, talk to a doctor before you use this service and make sure that vitamin D is safe for you:

  • high vitamin D levels
  • kidney stones (now or in the past)
  • too much parathyroid hormone (hyperparathyroidism)
  • cancer (some cancers can lead to high calcium levels)
  • severe kidney disease
  • a rare illness called sarcoidosis

Medicines

If you're taking any of these medicines, talk to a doctor before you use this service to make sure that vitamin D is safe for you:

  • digoxin, a medicine for heart problems
  • some diuretics (tablets which make you pee more) such as bendroflumethiazide
  • some medicines for epilepsy such as phenytoin, phenobarbital and primidone
  • medicines to treat tuberculosis (TB) such as rifampicin or isoniazid
  • medicines which affect how your body takes in fat, such as orlistat or cholestyramine
  • some antifungal medicines, such as ketoconazole or itraconazole
  • some steroids such as hydrocortisone or prednisolone, which are used to treat inflammation
  • actinomycin, a medicine used to treat some cancers

If you are not sure, read the full safety guidance on GOV.UK or check with a doctor.

Information:

If you have 1 of these medical conditions or are taking any of these medicines, you must only continue if your doctor tells you to.
